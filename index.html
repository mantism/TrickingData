What is Tricking?

"Martial arts tricking, known simply as tricking, is a training discipline that combines martial arts kicks with flips and twists from gymnastics as well as many dance moves and styles from breakdancing. It aims to achieve an aesthetic display of different combinations of "tricks". - Wikipedia "

Training

From just learning to backflip to landing their first double corkscrew, trickers put in various hours into their training in order to learn and perfect new skills. Of the 326 trickers that filled out the survey a majority indicated that they spend about two to eight hours a week tricking. Some outliers not explicitely expressed in the graph were trickers that indicated they put in 15, 18, and 20 hours a week into their tricking.

Aside from just numbers some interesting training habits were found in the survey. For example some responses were:
  • "Three sessions a week, tricking with my students for 4.5 hours a week, 4 hours of stretching weekly, 4 hours of resistance/corrective training."
  • "10-15 hours for sessions, lifting for tricking 6 times a week, trampoline training 1 hr"
  • "2h tricks 3h calisthenics"

Gender Gap

With the increase in popularity of gatherings like the semi-annual Euphoria Gathering hopefully one day this chart will represent a 50:50 ratio.

When you go to any trick session, attend any gathering, watch any tricking video, you will notice that the trickers are almost always male. Of the 326 trickers who filled out the survey, about 3% of the trickers are female. Unfortunately, this is pretty reflective of the rest of the tricking community. However, the number of female trickers in the community has grown over the years. In fact, groups like Euphoria Tricking and Intrepid Tricking have risen with the goal "to promote female tricking, to inspire, and motivate more females to start. "

Popular Tricks

Due to its eclectic origin there are a countless number of tricks being done these days and with new tricks being landed all the time its unclear where the limit is. So far the most popular "tricktionary" Club 540 contains 321 different tricks. However, when you account for different types of landings and take offs for tricks (an on going debate within the community) you have many more tricks and variations. Regardless, between the 326 surveyed trickers 65 different tricks were named when asked "What is your favorite trick?". Below you will find the top 10 most popular tricks as well the most popular kicks, flips, and twists amongst the responses. All information regarding trick's descriptions comes from Club540

Top 10* Most Popular Tricks

1. Corkscrew- 31 Favorites

Description: "A Corkscrew is a Btwist from a Moonkick approach. As the base leg pivots into the Moonkick, the hands are brought back towards the hips. Once the base leg is blocked in, the trail leg kicks across and upwards (much like kicking a soccer/football). As this happens, the practitioner jumps off of his base leg and wraps his arms across his body in the same direction as he kicked his trail leg. While wrapping, the legs are brought in tightly and the head turns in the appropriate direction to aid the twist. After 360° of rotation, the trick is landed in the same way as a Btwist would be. Like the Btwist, the Corkscrew is supposed to have flatspin of 180°."
Difficulty: Intermediate
Prerequisites: Gainer, Moonkick

2. Butterfly Twist - 17 Favorites

Description: "A trick in which a 360° twist is being executed while the body rotates 180° through the transverse plane (a flatspin). This trick is executed from the "butterfly" setup, and the wrap for the twist occurs at the same time you would kick up the back leg in the Butterfly Kick."
Difficulty: Beginner
Prerequisites: Butterfly Kick

3. Aerial - 16 Favorites

Description: "A cartwheel without hands. The Aerial is a trick in which the performer leans his upperbody over his base foot, and kicks up his back leg while launching off of his base foot. As the body gains lift, the leg that kicks back transfers to the other side of the body, causing the body to rotate along the coronal plane, and to make way for the landing. The chest should face the ground just before launching and just before landing the Aerial."
Difficulty: Novice
Prerequisites: Butterfly Kick

4. 540 - 15 Favorites

Description: "The first "cheat" trick. The 540 is a trick in which the performer executes a tornado kick and lands on the kicking leg. From a ready stance, the performer turns away from the target as if to perform an outside spin kick...instead, that action is faked, and the knee pulls upwards sharply as the performer jumps off his base leg. At this moment, a tornado kick is made ready; but instead of kicking it out like normal, the hips violently turn over and the kick is directed up and over in an arc-like fashion...leaving you to land on your kicking leg. Due to the nature of this kick, the soonest you can return to stance is after 540° of rotation from the initiation of the trick to the terminating of the same."
Difficulty: Novice
Prerequisites: Tornado Kick

5. Cheat 720 - 12 Favorites

Description: "The second "cheat" trick. The Cheat 720 is a trick where the performer executes an outside spin kick while spinning 540 degrees from the "cheat" setup. Simulating the Pop 720, the Cheat 720 still has 720 degrees of rotation from initial position to terminating position; however, only 540 degrees is performed in the air. The outside spin kick is fired off at the 360 degrees mark."
Difficulty: Beginner
Prerequisites: 540

6. Sideswipe - 11 Favorites

Description: "A Raiz Switch. An inverted 540. The Sideswipe occurs when an aggresive switch is used just before the spine aligns with the ground during the execution of a Raiz. This creates a swiping motion that denotes the characteristic of any "swipe" (a hypering inside spin kick done while inverted). This trick is landed in the same fashion as as a Flash Kick."
Difficulty: Intermediate
Prerequisites: 540, Raiz

6. Double Fulltwist - 11 Favorites

Description: "A flip with two full twists. In this trick, the performer jumps up and rotates 360 degrees backwards along the sagittal plane (or about the x-axis) while at the same time spinning 720 degrees along the transverse plane, before returning to his feet."
Difficulty: Advanced
Prerequisites: Fulltwist

6. Raiz - 11 Favorites

Description: "Commonly pronounced as "rise" or "raise", a Raiz is a Gumbi without hands. A trick where an Aerial is being kicked around versus being kicked back. The Raiz can also be depicted as a Gainer executed from the "cheat" setup."
Difficulty: Beginner
Prerequisites: Gumbi

7. Double Butterfly Twist - 8 Favorites

Description: "A Butterfly Twist with 720 degrees of rotation. Practitioner should muster a solid combination of height, speed, straight lined body positioning, and tight wrap in order to perform this technique."
Difficulty: Expert
Prerequisites: Butterfly Twist

7. Gainer Switch - 8 Favorites

Description: "The performer sets up into a gainer, swings their leg up, takes off and begins to somersault whilst kicking their foot over their head. At the peak of the jump the performer switches the leg that leads and lands back onto the takeoff leg."
Difficulty: Intermediate
Prerequisites: Backflip, Flash Kick, Gainer, Gainer Flash Kick

7. Jackknife- 8 Favorites

Description: "A 540 kick which has an outside hook or wheel kick thrown upon landing. Similar to cheat720 but with a hypered inside kick before the hook. Can sometimes be done with 2 chambered kicks as long as both are visible."
Difficulty: Intermediate
Prerequisites: 540, Cheat 720


7. Snapuswipe - 8 Favorites

Description: "Execute a sideswipe followed by a gyro."
Difficulty: Expert
Prerequisites: Sideswipe
